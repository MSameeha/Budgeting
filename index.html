Mike's Problem

Mike is 24 now. He wants to live independently but all his finances were
managed by his brother. Now that he will live independently he'll have to
do his own budgeting. Help Mike put his finances in order.

Basics of Finance Management

Firstly, Let's put the finances in order.Mike should allocate his money into clear buckets: mandatory expenses, discretionary expenses and savings.

Mandatory

Mandatory

Mandatory Expenses include house rent, transport, utilities, bill paying. Ideally it can cost upto 50% of total income after tax deduction.

Discretionary

Discretionary

These are in a simple language all Mike's 'wants'. Examples might include eating food outside, shopping, movies etc. Mike should keep in check his pattern of discretionary spending and do the cost too much.

Savings

Savings

It's not Mike's income that is important, it's the savings. Ideally one should save up to 20% of their total income. Then comes the concept of compounding and investing. Mike can invest in equity, bonds, real estate depending on his expected return value and risk management.
